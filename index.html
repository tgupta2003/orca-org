ORCAS

PLAN TO PROTECT

Orcas, found in all oceans and most seas in the world, are vital in keeping regions’ ecosystems intact. They play a significant role in keeping marine-life population balanced and dispersing nutrients in oceans and seas. These intelligent, quick, social creatures have unfortunately been taken into captivity since 1961 — disrupting their natural social groupings. Since then, they’ve been used for commercial and research purposes, with over 176 dying in captivity. Orcas in the waters surrounding British Columbia, Washington and Oregon, are now regarded as an endangered population — with numbers dropping each year.

Join us in supporting the release of orcas in captivity back into the wild where they deserve a chance to live, grow, prosper in their rightful habitats.
